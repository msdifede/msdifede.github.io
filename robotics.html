Robotics - Connecting an NXT Sensor

The Physical Connection

The HiTechnic sensors are made for connecting to an NXT or EV3 system. Because of this, they have the RJ12 plugs. These plugs are very similar to an ethernet or phone connection. Additionally, this plug may be very familiar to you if you ever progammed Lego robotics.

Connect the breadboard to your robot with Velcro: Cut a small piece of velcro to attach to the breadboard and the top of your robot. This will secure the breadboard so we can use it to run the wires. This setup is great for getting started quickly, but in a competition, you would want a more secure sensor connection.

Add a Sensor Mount made from Legos. On yimagesour robot, there is a Tetrix hardpoint connector which allows you to connect lego pieces to the robot. Using pegs and beams, create a mount which puts the sensor at the angle and position where you think it will get the best readings. Because this is made with Legos, you can easily modify it in the future.

The Electrical Connection

Since our robot does not have NXT compatible sockets, we need a way to connect the sensors. We can split open the RJ12 cable and connect the wires directly into the Arduino.

  1. Cut an NXT Wire in half: Use a wire cutter to cut an NXT wire in half
  2. Expose Individual Wires: Using a wire cutter and a wire stripper cut and peel back the black insulating wire to expose the 6 individual color wires. Strip the end of each wire approximately 1/4 inch.
  3. Connect the Wire: You need to connect the individual wire to the ports below. To connect, you try putting the wire directly into the proto-shield, or solder to additional male wires to get a connection into a breadboard.
    • white : Vin
    • black : GND
    • red : GND
    • green : 3.3V
    • yellow : Arduino SCL
    • blue : Arduino SDA
  4. Secure and Label: Make sure all the wires are secure and in the right place in the Arduino and the breadboard. It is a great time to make sure each wire is labeled with a small piece of tape. You can write “Vin Pin1” on the tape, so that if the wire becomes unplugged, you can easily replace it.
